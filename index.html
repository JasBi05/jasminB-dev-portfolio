About Me

Raised in Germany, Berlin, I developed an early interest in video games. Whether it was Nintendo, SEGA, or more serious games from
CD Projekt, my taste has always been diverse.

It wasn't just the story that appealed to me:
I was also curious about how a game is produced from start to finish.

That sense of curiosity stayed with me into adulthood. In October 2024, I began studying International Media and Computing at
University of Applied Sciences (HTW) Berlin, and I am expected to graduate in 2027.

I have studied a wide range of topics, from web development to Python programming. Furthermore, I worked in teams and was introduced to Scrum. Through this experience, I realized that contribute to a team in an empathetic, analytical, and creative way.

Color Clash

Language: Python
Program: PyCharm

Inspired by Splatoon, players compete to cover as much area as possible. The player controlling the largest portion wins.

The idea emerged from an unexpected behavior while learning Python. A rendering issue created a persistent coloring effect, which evolved into the core mechanic.
